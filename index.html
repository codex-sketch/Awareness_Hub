What is Feminism?

Feminism is the belief in social, political, and economic equality of the sexes. It advocates for equal rights, opportunities, and respect for all genders. Feminism is not about putting one gender above another; it‚Äôs about fairness, balance, and justice.

Understanding Misandry

Misandry is the dislike, prejudice, or hatred toward men. It often arises from misunderstanding or negative experiences but, like misogyny, it promotes division rather than equality. True feminism rejects misandry because its goal is fairness, not revenge.

Feminism vs Misandry

Many people confuse Feminism with Misandry. Below is a simple comparison to clarify the difference:

Genuine feminism does not promote hatred, bitterness, or injustice towards men. True feminism stands for equity over equality ‚Äî recognizing that fairness comes from understanding differences, not ignoring them. It ensures that everyone, regardless of gender, has access to the opportunities and respect they deserve.
